Homeowners Insurance

Homeowners insurance provides financial protection against disasters. A standard policy insures the home itself and the things you keep in it.

Homeowners Insurance

Homeowners insurance is a package policy. This means that it covers both damage to your property and your liability or legal responsibility for any injuries and property damage you or members of your family cause to other people. This includes damage caused by household pets.

Damage caused by most disasters is covered but there are exceptions. The most significant are damage caused by floods, earthquakes and poor maintenance. Earthquake Coverage is offered by Houston General Insurance but you must buy a separate policy for flood coverage. Maintenance-related problems are the homeowners' responsibility.

Homeowners Coverage

Typically, your home is your largest investment. Is it well protected? Will you have enough coverage if it is destroyed by fire, lightning, tornado, or man-made events such as burglary, vandalism or arson? Homeowners policies have been sold in the United States since the 1950's and today are in very common use throughout the country.

A homeowner policy usually has several separate coverages. Click on each tab to learn more.

Dwelling Coverage

This portion of a homeowner policy covers the structure itself. It is most important that you cover your house with enough coverage to replace it if it were totally destroyed. Remember that rebuilding one house is different than building a subdivision for the first time. For example, the reconstruction effort after a fire usually starts with the debris removal. Sometimes access to the property is now much more difficult than when originally built.

Therefore, let an independent agent help you determine what the correct value of your home is for replacement coverage. There is enough difficulty in starting over after a catastrophic loss such as a tornado for example, but do not make matters worse by having an inadequate replacement cost coverage amount of insurance.

Other Structures

This coverage will help replace or pay for damages caused to physical structures not attached to your house. This may include but not limited to a detached garage, shed, fence and utility line you own.

Personal Property

All the goods and furniture in your home constitute contents. But all policies are not alike and some contents are limited in specific value due to the nature of the item or use. For example, business property is often limited to a specific dollar amount. Remember that replacement of your contents requires replacement coverage; otherwise you will only be afforded Actual Cash Value (ACV), which is the replacement cost of the item minus depreciation.

Let an independent agent compare your existing coverage with other programs and give you expert advice on how best to meet your needs. When insuring high value items such as jewelry watches and furs, be sure to ask your agent about special policy limitations for these types of property.

Personal Liability

Accidents happen at home and when your guest is injured due to your negligence, the homeowner liability section responds. For example, a neighbor is invited over and trips over the garden hose you left out. This is an event that your liability coverage provides coverage for.

Likewise, liability can extend away from your premises as well for activities you or family members are involved with. An important part of the liability protection is defending you from lawsuits for incidents taking place on your premise. Defense costs themselves can be significant and are included in your homeowner protection.

Loss of Use

If your home were destroyed by fire, where would you live? How would you pay for temporary expenses? This portion of your homeowners policy provides for the additional cost of living when it is necessary for you to leave home due to a covered loss. As with all sections of the homeowners policy make sure you have enough coverage. Let an independent agent help you determine what is right for you.

Optional Coverages

Water Sewer Back-up

With so many benefits of being a homeowner, there are also many responsibilities. Did you know that as a homeowner you are not only responsible for the maintenance and repair of your home, but also of the sewer lines extending from your home to the city or county’s main sewer line as well?

There are many causes of sewer backups such as blockage from tree roots or malfunction from the Sump Pump causing a water backup which may overflow your toilets causing damage to your home.

These damages can be extensive and very costly. How would you pay for the repairs to your home and it’s contents? Water sewer backup is excluded on your policy but for an additional premium you can purchase this coverage to assist you in the restoration of your home and it’s contents.

Scheduled Personal Property

While Personal Property covers your personal property from a covered loss, there are limitations to certain property. Whether you have an art or gun collection, or perhaps you want to insure that sentimental engagement ring, we offer both scheduled and unscheduled coverage for your valuables. Be sure to ask your agent about special policy limitations for these types of property and increase where desired for that peace of mind.

Home Cyber Protection

It is not just large business and organization that are affected by cybercrime, but individuals like you and me. We live in an era where more individuals turn to the world-wide web to connect or perhaps to complete their Holiday shopping. As more users connect online, the greater the risk of a cyber-attack.

Frequently Asked Questions

What types of properties does HGI insure?
HGI insures single family homes or townhomes, condos or apartments (HO3, HO6, and HO4 respectively) No more than two units in a contiguous structure ie. Condos.

What types of properties are eligible for HGI?
HGI is not a market for secondary or seasonal dwellings (unless approved by underwriting). HGI is not a market for First-Tier dwellings, dwellings within 300 feet of any brush exposure, or dwellings that are not visible from a neighboring residence. Protection class 10 or unprotected dwellings are ineligible for coverage with HGI.

Are homes eligible for coverage with HGI that have an in-home business exposure?
HGI is not a market for dwellings with a business exposure that involves customer or employee traffic. This includes child day care and elder care. In-home office and very limited customer traffic is acceptable.

Is HGI okay with insuring dwelling with acreage?
Up to 81 acres is eligible for coverage (no farms)

What are HGI’s maximum and minimum Homeowners (HO3) (Coverage A) requirements?
The maximum replacement cost is $600,000. The minimum replacement cost is $100,000.

What are my binding authority limits?
Our Underwriting Systems are highly automated such that our acceptance or rejection of an account is usually known to the user immediately. With this type of automation, we want to be sure that there is no authorization to bind outside of the automated process unless there is express written authorization from Houston General Insurance Exchange.

Further to this point, we reserve the right to suspend all activity on our underwriting screens when and where conditions warrant.

What is HGI’s Insurance to Value philosophy?
For our Homeowners (HO3), we expect all dwellings to be written/insured at 100% of the dwelling replacement value (nothing less than the indicated insurance to value for 100% replacement cost). The maximum coverage for contents and building items on the Condominium (HO6) is $500,000. The Minimum coverage for contents on the Renters (HO4) is $25,000.

What claims/prior losses should be included on the homeowner’s quote?
The HGI prior loss experience period is 5 years (including CAT losses).

Does HGI inspect home?
HGI does inspect homes based on Underwriting discretion.

Are Pools and Trampolines acceptable?
Pools with diving boards are ineligible. Trampolines without side safety netting are ineligible.

Are wood roofs eligible for coverage?
HGI is not a market for dwellings with a wood shingle roof.

Are dwelling roof losses/claims settled at replacement cost?
Losses for roofs exceeding 30 years of age will be settled on an actual cash value basis.

Are log homes eligible for coverage with HGI?
HGI is not a market for solid log home. However, log facing construction is acceptable.

Are dwellings with asbestos siding or an asbestos roof eligible for coverage?
A dwelling with asbestos siding or an asbestos roof is ineligible for coverage with HGI.

Are dwellings with knob-and-tube wiring or fuse boxes eligible for coverage?
Dwellings with knob-and-tube wiring or fuse boxes are ineligible for coverage. All dwellings must be updated to code.

Can a home that is titled/owned in the name of a LLC or LLP be insured with HGI?
Please contact your underwriter for consideration.

Is a home that is titled/owned in the name of a Trust eligible for coverage with HGI?
Please contact your Underwriter for consideration.

Is HGI a market for dwellings with an above ground propane/oil tank located on the premises?
No. Properties with an above ground propane/oil tank are ineligible for coverage with HGI.
