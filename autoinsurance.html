Auto Insurance

Auto insurance protects you against financial loss if you have an accident. It is a contract between you and the insurance company. You agree to pay the premium and the insurance company agrees to pay your losses as defined in your policy.

Auto Insurance Coverage Options

An auto insurance policy provides both vital and convenience coverages. Differentiating between the two is not always clear. Allow a Houston General Insurance Agent to educate you on policy coverages to avoid any unpleasant surprises.

Your auto insurance policy is comprised of the following basic coverages:

A. Bodily Injury and Property Damage Liability
B. Medical Payments Coverage or Personal Injury Protection
C. Uninsured Motorist/Underinsured Motorist (UMBIPD/UIMBIPD)
D. Comprehensive – Other than Collision
E. Collision Coverage

The following Optional Coverages are available:

• Rental Reimbursement
• Roadside Assistance
• Auto Loan Lease

Texas law requires all drivers to carry Liability Insurance, Uninsured Motorist/Underinsured Motorist Coverage and Personal Injury Protection unless rejected in writing. If you are financing a car, your lender may also have requirements.

Bodily Injury and Property Damage Liability

Auto liability insurance protects you from accidental damages caused by you to other people or property. It is typically sold in increments of per person / per accident bodily injury and property damage. For example, 100/300/100 provides $100,000 in coverage for each injured person up to a maximum of $300,000 per accident for all bodily injuries. There is also coverage in this example for $100,000 in property damage.

Medical Payments Coverage or Personal Injury Protection

Medical payments coverage provides medical expenses coverage for you and your passengers. This coverage can be used to treat or diagnose injuries resulting from an accident involving your vehicle. The idea is that some level of protection ought to exist whether you are at fault or not. Like Medical Payments Insurance, Personal Injury Protection will pay for reasonable medical expenses resulting from an automobile collision. Additionally, it will pay up to 80% of lost wages and the cost associated with hiring a caregiver for an injured person up to the limit on your policy. Texas requires insurance companies to offer this coverage unless it is rejected in writing by the insured.

Uninsured Motorist/Underinsured Motorist (UMBIPD/IUMPBID)

Suppose you are involved in an accident that is not your fault, will you have enough coverage to carry on your life as before? Will the other party who is at fault have any insurance or enough? Statistics show that in about 14% of all accidents the at–fault party will not have any insurance. The chances are even greater that the at-fault party will not have enough to take care of your injuries or your passengers. Not only is Uninsured/Underinsured Motorist Coverage important to take care of you and your family, it is a required coverage unless rejected in writing.

Other than Collision (Comprehensive Coverage)

Would a hail storm's damage be covered under my auto policy? Yes, if you have comprehensive coverage – which covers perils such as flood, fire, theft, vandalism, glass breakage, etc. Many things can happen to your car while it is not being operated. Protect your car with Other Than Collision Coverage. It is one of the most frequently used coverages. A rock hitting and breaking a windshield, a flash flood washing away your car, or a deer running and colliding with your vehicle are all types of Comprehensive Claims.

Collision Coverage

Would my car be repaired after an accident? Collision coverage provides basic protection for your vehicle if it is involved in an accident. If you have an accident, Collision Coverage will protect your car even if another driver is at fault or has no insurance. You must have comprehensive coverage to purchase Collision protection. Rental Reimbursement

In the event of an accident Rental Reimbursement Coverage covers the cost of renting a vehicle while your vehicle is being repaired. This coverage is subject to all policy provisions.

Auto Loan Lease

When involved in a total loss accident or theft this coverage provides peace of mind by paying for the difference between the amount left on your loan or lease agreement and the depreciated value of your vehicle. Also referred to as GAP coverage.

Frequently Asked Questions

Does HGI offer a 6-month private passenger auto policy?

Yes. A 6 month or 12 month auto policy is available. Certain risks will only qualify for a 6 month policy.

Do all driver’s in the household need to be listed on the policy?
Yes. All licensed drives living in the household must be listed as rated or excluded.

Do drivers with a learner’s permit need to be listed on the policy?
No. Drivers with a learner’s permit do not need to be listed on the policy until they obtain a valid driver’s U.S. or Canadian driver’s license.

Do students away at school need to be listed on the policy?
Yes. Students away at should are considered household members and need to be rated or excluded on the policy.

Is HGI a market for vehicles garaged out of state?
No. The garaging address for all listed vehicles must be in the state of Texas. The billing address can be located outside of the state of Texas.

Are drivers with an international driver’s license eligible for an auto policy with HGI?
No. All operators must have a valid U.S. or Canadian driver’s license. A license from another country is no sufficient.

Are drivers requiring a SR22 (certificate of financially responsible) eligible for coverage with HGI?
No. Any operator with a requirement to produce a SR 22 is ineligible for coverage with HGI.

Are drivers with a suspended or revoked driver’s license eligible for coverage?
No. Any driver that has a suspended or revoked drivers’ license is not eligible for coverage with HGI.

What driving activity needs to be included?
Accidents and violations in the past five years need to be included for any rated driver on the policy. (excluding towing losses).

How are drivers assigned to vehicles?
Drivers should be assigned principal operator of the vehicle they primarily operate. However, when the driver to vehicle ratio is not equal, all youthful operators should be assigned principal operator of a listed vehicle prior to assigning any adult drivers to a vehicle.

Does HGI insure limited production or exotic vehicles?
No. HGI is not a market for exotic or limited production vehicles. In addition, HGI is not a market for RV’s, motorhomes, off road vehicles, ATV’s, kit cars, motorcycles, motor bikes, scooters, or grey market vehicles.

Are pickup trucks eligible for coverage with HGI?
Pickup trucks are acceptable up to ¾ ton. A full one ton is not acceptable.

Are salvaged title vehicles eligible for coverage with HGI?
No. HGI is not a market for salvaged titled vehicles.

Is business use of a vehicle acceptable on HGI personal lines private passenger policy?
Vehicles used for livery services such as Uber, Lyft, etc. are ineligible for coverage with HGI. Artisan use is acceptable. Vehicles used for pick-up and or deliver ie. pizza or newspaper delivery are ineligible for coverage with HGI.

What are my binding authority limits?
Our Underwriting Systems are highly automated such that our acceptance or rejection of an account is usually known to the user immediately. With this type of automation, we want to be sure that there is no authorization to bind outside of the automated process unless there is express written authorization from Houston General Insurance Exchange.

Further to this point, we reserve the right to suspend all activity on our underwriting screens when and where conditions warrant.
